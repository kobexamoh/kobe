The Formal Stuff

That wasn't too bad, but I missed school (believe it or not). I went back to school to study Digital Media and Information Technology, and I'm slated to graduate in spring 2020.

I've worked at (tiny) startups and media companies, and cut my teeth at big academic institutions, too. I'm currently looking for internships and roles around front-end work at organizations that care about diversity, inclusion, and don't mind if their people come from non-traditional backgrounds (because that's me!).

If this is you, send me a note: I'd love to get to know you better.

The Informal Stuff

Outside of being a student, I do a lot. I'm a writer: I have my own blog where I write once a week or so about life.

I'm also a photographer that focuses on lifestyle and commercial work. I've shot work for politicians (former premier Rachel Notley), for brands and their affiliates (Adidas Canada + Top 40 Basketball Camps), and advertising/commercial work, too (Tourism Red Deer; City of Leduc).

I do community stuff as well. I'm the website manager for UrbanYEG, a local community group that combines photography and social groups as a way to bring people together and raise support for mental health initiatives.

Physical activity is my jam: I've played soccer football since I was more or less walking. If you're in need of an attacking midfielder or a winger, holler at me and I'll be happy to assist your goals.

If not that, you'll find me busting weights in a gym, running around the community (I like a good medium distance run in the evenings), or longboarding with my mates in search of good photoshoot locations. The usual, really.
